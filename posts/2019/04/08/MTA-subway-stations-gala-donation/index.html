Maximizing Donations for A Tech Company in NYC

Tech Company targeting its Gala Signups... at subway stations?

New York City is an ultimate American city: A melting pot of all cultures that presents itself to exciting opportunities; all that you can possibly see and experience in the rest of the United States, you will likely to do so in New York. One of the most iconic institutions in NYC is its 115-year-old subway system. I can almost guarantee that everyone born in New York City has at one point in their life taken the subway to go somewhere and do something. The story for today takes us to the very New York Subway Stations.

The Story: WomenTechWomenYes

WomenTechWomenYes is a fictional company that promotes passion and participation of women in technology. WTWY is hosting a gala and needs attendees. One of its fundraising efforts is to employ its street teams at NYC subway stations for email sign ups; signees receive free tickets to the gala where they are encouraged to donate to raising the awareness of women in technology.

The company has tasked us, a team of 2, to help optimize the placement of the street team, to be able to gather the most signatures, ideally from those who will attend the gala and contribute.

I, along with my partner, used the public MTA subway data and applied Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to come up with our suggestions. EDA was done using Python and include the basic data science libraries (pandas, seaborn).
